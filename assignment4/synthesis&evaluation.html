Appendix A

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines

Guidelines Description
Guideline 1.1 All non-text content has an alternative text in case of errors. Non-text Content - ‘alt’ is included in all ‘img’ tags.
Guideline 1.3 Width and height measurements of our contents were specified in viewport units to keep the readable layout of our webpage with changing screensize. Info and Relationships – information is displayed in text understandably. Meaningful Sequence – sequence of our text is very clear. Sensory Characteristics – our content does not rely on sensory characteristics, because we use viewport unites to display our content.
Guideline 1.4 Foreground and background are separated so that it is visually comfortable to see. Use of Color – text and color are two visual means of conveying information in our website. Audio Control – Our website does not contain any audio. Our website fits the requirement of text and image of text contrast ratio of at least 4,5:1. Resize text – text in our website can be resized up to 200 percent without loss of content or functionality. Image of Text - images of text can be visually customized because of our responsive design. Contrast (Enhanced) - the visual presentation of text and images of text in our website has a contrast ratio of at least 7:1. Low or No Background Audio – Our website does not contain any audio. Visual Presentation – Foreground and background colors cannot be selected by the user. The width is no more than 80 characters. Text is aligned to both the left and the right margins. Line spacing is default. Text is resized in a way that user does not have to scroll horizontally to read a line of text on a full-screen window. Images of Text (No Exception) - Images of text are used for decoration.
Guideline 2.1 Some functionality of a website is accessible thought keyboard. Keyboard – Some functionality like input could be operated through a keyboard interface, but mouse is the best tool for navigating the website. No Keyboard Trap – Keyboard focus can be moved to a component of the page using a keyboard interface. Keyboard (No Exception) - Some functionality like inputs should be operated through a keyboard, but mouse works the best on navigating the website.
Guideline 2.4 Website provides ways to help users to navigate, find content and determine where they are in the website. Bypass Blocks – information is not repeated on our website. Page Titled – Our web page have titles and descriptions. Focus Order – The navigation of our website is sequential, and the order of our components is easy to follow and operate. Link Purpose (In Context) - The purpose of our links can be easily understandable from the text alone. Multiple Ways – Our website has only a single Web page. Headings and Labels – Headings and labels clearly describe topics on our website. Focus Visible – Keyboard use in our input form is clearly visible. Location - The location on the web page of a user is clearly visible. Link Purpose (link only) - Link purpose is very understandable from the link text. Section Headings – Headings are used to organize the content on our website.
